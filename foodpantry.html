University of Michigan Food Pantry

The University of Michigan Food Pantry is dedicated to supporting students, faculty, and staff who are experiencing food insecurity. Our mission is to ensure that every member of the U-M community has access to nutritious food, essential supplies, and resources to help them thrive academically and personally.

Supporting Our Community One Meal at a Time at the Maize & Blue Cupboard

Here's How It Works:

  1. Make an appointment: Shopping at the Maize and Blue Cupboard is by appointment only. You can sign up for a visit two weeks in advance.
  2. Visit: Come during your scheduled appointed time. Be quick! You will only have 15 minutes to shop. We want to make sure we accommodate every student in need.
  3. Enjoy: We provide food, kitchen and cooking products, personal and household items and general support resources to ensure all bases are covered for you.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who can use the food pantry?

The food pantry is open to all University of Michigan students, faculty, and staff who are experiencing food insecurity.

What should I bring when I visit the pantry?

Bring your MCard and any reusable bags you have for carrying your items.

How often can I visit the pantry?

You can make appointments and visit the pantry based on upon your own personal needs. We just ask that you take what you need and eat what you take.

Will this affect my financial aid?

No, accessing Maize and Blue Cupboard will not impact your financial aid.
